Understanding the Master Shot Sequence

What is a Master Shot?

In traditional filmmaking, the master shot is a continuous wide shot that captures the entire scene from beginning to end. It serves as:

üé¨

Visual Foundation

Establishes where everyone is and their spatial relationships

Editing Safety Net

Provides coverage you can always cut back to if other angles fail

üé≠

Performance Reference

Shows the full action and timing of the scene

Continuity Anchor

The source of truth for what happens when

In AI Filmmaking:

Because AI tools limit us to short clips (5-10 seconds), we can't generate the entire master shot in one go. Instead, we "chain" multiple clips together, using the last frame of one clip as the starting image for the next.

Why Chain Instead of Generate Separately?

  • Visual Continuity: The last frame ensures the next clip starts exactly where the previous one ended
  • Smooth Motion: No jarring jumps or discontinuities between segments
  • Character Consistency: Expressions and positions flow naturally
  • Professional Result: Looks like one continuous shot when edited together

Scene Organization Tips:

  • Name your files clearly: "master_segment_01.mp4", "master_segment_02.mp4", etc.
  • Save all last frames: "master_frame_01.jpg", "master_frame_02.jpg", etc.

Common Issues & Solutions

Problem: Lip-Sync is Off

Solution: Add "accurate lip-sync" and "natural mouth movement" to prompt. Consider shortening dialogue.

Problem: Characters Move Too Much

Solution: Add "minimal movement" or "subtle gestures" to keep them relatively still.

Problem: Camera Drifts

Solution: Emphasize "keep camera completely static" or "locked-off camera" in every prompt.

Problem: Clip Looks Different

Solution: Make sure you're using the LAST FRAME of previous clip, not the master image.

Problem: Awkward Pauses

Solution: Add "Character A finishes speaking, Character B immediately responds" to prompt.

Problem: Wrong Emotion

Solution: Specify emotion: "Character A says angrily" or "Character B responds sadly".

üîß Troubleshooting Mindset

AI generation is iterative. If a segment doesn't work, regenerate it with a refined prompt. Don't chain bad clips‚Äîfix them first! You can always go back to the previous good frame and try again.

Advanced Prompting Tips

Voice & Performance Direction

The AI responds well to performance directions:

  • Voice Quality: "Character A speaks in a deep, steady male voice"
  • Pacing: "Character B responds quickly" or "long pause before speaking"
  • Emotion: "says nervously" / "responds with confidence" / "whispers"
  • Volume: "raises voice" / "speaks softly"

Enhanced Prompt Example:

Continuing from this frame, the man says in a calm, measured voice, "I'm breaking up with you." Brief pause. The woman's expression shifts from confusion to shock. Natural reactions, subtle movements, accurate lip-sync, keep camera static.

Note how this adds performance detail without overcomplicating the visual description.

In-Class Exercise

Generate Your First Two Segments

  1. Load your Master Image into your AI tool
  2. Generate Segment 1 using your first script segment
  3. Review the result - does it work?
  4. Extract the last frame
  5. Generate Segment 2 from that last frame
  6. Watch both clips back-to-back

‚úÖ Success Criteria

  • Lip-sync matches the dialogue reasonably well
  • Characters maintain consistent appearance
  • Camera stays static (no unwanted movement)
  • Transition between clips is smooth
  • Dialogue timing feels natural

What to Do If It's Not Working:

  • Lip-sync issues: Simplify dialogue, add "accurate lip-sync" to prompt
  • Too much movement: Add "minimal movement, subtle gestures"
  • Jarring transition: Make sure you used the LAST frame, not a middle frame
  • Wrong emotion: Be more specific about performance in prompt

Don't move forward with bad clips! Regenerate until you have two solid segments.
