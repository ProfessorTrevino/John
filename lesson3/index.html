The Critical Principle

"Do not chain angles. Always return to the source."

üéØ This is THE Most Important Rule

For EVERY new camera angle, you MUST start from the original master image.

Never use a frame from your master shot sequence as the starting point for a new angle. Never chain one angle into another angle.

After Generating Segment 1:

Now you can chain this specific angle's segments together, just like you did with the master shot.

  1. Extract last frame of OTS Segment 1
  2. Use that frame as input for OTS Segment 2
  3. Prompt: "Continuing from this over-the-shoulder frame, [next dialogue]"
  4. Repeat until the OTS angle covers your full scene

The Spatial Confusion Problem

‚ö†Ô∏è AI's Biggest Weakness

Current AI tools frequently misinterpret directional commands like "left," "right," "over left shoulder," "pan right," etc.

The Left/Right Problem

When you say "over the left shoulder," the AI might interpret this as:

Camera-Left?

Left side of the frame

Scene-Left?

Stage left in the scene

Character's Left?

The character's actual left shoulder

Viewer's Left?

Left from audience perspective

Result: The AI guesses randomly and is often wrong!

‚úì THE SOLUTION: Avoid "Left" and "Right"

Instead of using directional words, use descriptive positioning.

‚ùå BAD PROMPTS

  • "Move over the left shoulder"
  • "Pan right to close-up"
  • "OTS on the right"
  • "Camera moves to the left side"

Why they fail: Ambiguous directional terms

‚úì GOOD PROMPTS

  • "Position camera behind Character A, looking over Character A's shoulder to frame Character B's face"
  • "Push in to a tight close-up on Character A's face"
  • "Re-frame to a medium shot of Character B with Character A's shoulder in foreground"
  • "Move camera to new position behind Character B"

Why they work: Descriptive, unambiguous positioning

Complete Workflow Summary

Your Scene Coverage Workflow

‚úì You Already Have:

  • Master Image (your source)
  • Complete Master Shot Sequence (chained segments)
  • Script broken into segments

üìã For Each New Angle:

  1. Start from original master image
  2. Generate Segment 1 with camera move + first dialogue
  3. Extract last frame of Segment 1
  4. Generate Segment 2 from that last frame
  5. Continue chaining until angle is complete
  6. Return to step 1 for next angle

üé¨ Angles to Generate:

  • OTS Character B (full scene length)
  • OTS Character A (full scene length)
  • Close-Up Character A (key moments)
  • Close-Up Character B (reactions)
  • Optional: Medium shots, inserts, etc.

‚ö° Key Reminders

  • CRITICAL Every new angle starts from the original master image
  • CRITICAL Avoid "left/right" commands‚Äîuse descriptive positioning
  • Chain segments within the same angle
  • Never chain from one angle to another angle
  • Use the same dialogue for all angles (ensures sync in editing)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå

Mistake #1

Using a master shot frame as input for OTS

Always return to the original master image for each new angle!

‚ùå

Mistake #2

Using "left" or "right" in prompts

AI will likely misinterpret. Use descriptive positioning instead.

‚ùå

Mistake #3

Chaining OTS into close-up

Each angle gets its own chain starting from the master image.

‚ùå

Mistake #4

Different dialogue for different angles

Keep dialogue identical across angles so they sync in editing.

‚ùå

Mistake #5

Forgetting "From this original image"

This phrase helps anchor the AI to your source image.

‚ùå

Mistake #6

Too much camera movement in one prompt

Keep moves simple: wide to OTS, or OTS held steady.

In-Class Exercise

Generate Your First OTS Angle

Today, you'll generate at least the first two segments of ONE over-the-shoulder angle.

  1. Load your original master image (not a master shot frame!)
  2. Write your OTS prompt using descriptive positioning (no "left/right")
  3. Generate OTS Segment 1
  4. Evaluate: Does the camera angle look right? Is continuity maintained?
  5. If good: Extract last frame
  6. If bad: Refine prompt and regenerate
  7. Generate OTS Segment 2 from the last frame
  8. Review: Does it chain smoothly?

‚úÖ Success Criteria

  • Camera is clearly in OTS position
  • Characters match the master image (faces, clothes, location)
  • Dialogue syncs reasonably well
  • Transition between Segment 1 and 2 is smooth

Troubleshooting Guide

  • Wrong angle generated: Be more specific in positioning description
  • Characters look different: Make sure you used the ORIGINAL master image, not a master shot frame
  • Camera in wrong place: Avoid "left/right," describe the exact position
  • Too much movement: Add "hold this angle" or "maintain this shot"
